🌙 Hajj for Kids

Learn about Hajj, its beautiful rites, important places, and what Muslims around the world do during these blessed days.

📖 What Is Hajj?

Hajj is a special pilgrimage Muslims make to Mecca in Saudi Arabia. It is the 5th Pillar of Islam and is required once in a lifetime for Muslims who are able. Millions of Muslims gather together to worship Allah, seek forgiveness, and remember the story of Prophet Ibrahim (AS) and his family.

📗 Quran Verse About Hajj

"And Hajj to the House is a duty that mankind owes to Allah, for those who are able to undertake the journey."

— Surah Aal 'Imran (3:97)

📜 Hadith About Hajj

"Whoever performs Hajj for Allah and does not behave badly or commit sins will return like the day his mother gave birth to him."

— Sahih al-Bukhari

🌟 Why Muslims Perform Hajj

  • To obey Allah
  • To seek forgiveness
  • To strengthen faith
  • To remember Prophet Ibrahim (AS)
  • To unite with Muslims around the world

🧕 Clothing of Hajj

Men: Wear simple white cloth called Ihram.


Women: Wear modest Islamic clothing.


Pilgrims avoid arguing and focus on worship and good manners.

🌍 Important Places in Hajj

🕋 Kaaba: The cube-shaped building Muslims face when praying.
⛺ Mina: A place filled with white tents where pilgrims stay during Hajj.
⛰️ Mount Arafat: The place where pilgrims make dua and ask Allah for forgiveness.
🌙 Muzdalifah: The area where pilgrims sleep outdoors and collect pebbles.

🤲 What Muslims Not Performing Hajj Can Do

    📅 Days of Eid and the Days of Tashreeq (10th–13th of Dhul Hijjah)

    🕌 10th of Dhul Hijjah — Eid al-Adha

    The 10th day of Dhul Hijjah is Eid al-Adha, one of the two major Islamic celebrations. Muslims around the world celebrate this blessed day by worshipping Allah, praying Eid prayer, remembering the obedience of Prophet Ibrahim (AS), and helping others.

    What Muslims Not Performing Hajj Can Do

    • Wake up early for Eid
    • Perform ghusl (ritual bath) before Eid prayer
    • Wear clean or nice clothes
    • Say the Takbeer:
    "Allahu Akbar, Allahu Akbar, Laa ilaaha illallaah, Allahu Akbar, Allahu Akbar wa lillaahil hamd"
    • Attend the Eid prayer
    • Listen to the khutbah (sermon)
    • Perform Qurbani/Udhiyah if able
    • Share meat with family, neighbors, and the needy
    • Visit relatives and friends
    • Give charity and gifts
    • Make du'a and thank Allah

    🌙 11th–13th of Dhul Hijjah — The Days of Tashreeq

    The 11th, 12th, and 13th of Dhul Hijjah are called the Days of Tashreeq. These are blessed days of worship, remembrance of Allah, happiness, eating, and gratitude.

    What Muslims Can Do During These Days

    • Continue saying the Takbeer after the daily prayers
    • Remember Allah often
    • Read Quran
    • Make du'a
    • Spend time with family
    • Share food and meat from Qurbani
    • Help the poor and needy
    • Thank Allah for His blessings

    Important Note

    The Prophet ﷺ described these as days of:

    • eating
    • drinking
    • and remembering Allah

    Muslims should continue worshipping Allah and showing good manners throughout these blessed days.
